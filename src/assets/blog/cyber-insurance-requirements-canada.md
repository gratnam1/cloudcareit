# Cyber Insurance Requirements for Canadian Businesses in 2026

If your business in Canada has applied for or renewed a cyber insurance policy recently, you've likely seen the massive multi-page questionnaires. Insurers are no longer handing out policies just because you asked. They are verifying that you have specific cybersecurity controls in place.

Here is what your cyber insurance provider requires in 2026 before they issue a policy or pay out a claim.

## 1. Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Everywhere
MFA is no longer optional. Insurers require MFA to be enforced on:
- All email accounts (Microsoft 365, Google Workspace)
- All remote access tools (VPNs, RDP)
- All administrative accounts and financial software

## 2. Endpoint Detection and Response (EDR)
Legacy antivirus is dead. Insurers mandate next-generation EDR (like Huntress or SentinelOne) that offers behavioral analysis, 24/7 monitoring, and isolation capabilities to stop threats like ransomware before they encrypt your network.

## 3. Verified, Isolated Backups
Backups must be isolated from the primary network. If ransomware hits your network, it cannot be able to delete your backups. Insurers look for:
- **Immutable Backups:** Backups that cannot be altered or deleted.
- **Offsite Storage:** Cloud backups securely stored away from the office.
- **Regular Testing:** Proof that restores are tested frequently.

## 4. Managed Patching and Vulnerability Management
You must prove that your operating systems, applications, and firewalls are patched promptly against known vulnerabilities. Relying on users to "Click Update" is not sufficient.

## 5. Security Awareness Training
Human error remains the #1 cause of breaches. Policies often require documented, ongoing security awareness training and phishing simulations for all employees.
