# How to Protect Your GTA Business from Ransomware in 2026

Ransomware attacks against small and medium businesses in the Greater Toronto Area are at an all-time high. Hackers are no longer just asking for a few hundred dollars; they are demanding thousands, stealing sensitive data, and threatening to leak it online if you don't pay.

Here is exactly how you can protect your business from ransomware.

## 1. Stop Using Legacy Antivirus -> Upgrade to EDR
Traditional antivirus only looks for known signatures of old viruses. Modern ransomware bypasses it completely. You need **Endpoint Detection and Response (EDR)**. Solutions like Huntress monitor the actual *behavior* of programs on your computers and automatically isolate an infected machine the moment it starts acting suspicious.

## 2. Enforce Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
The majority of ransomware attacks start with a compromised password. An employee re-uses a password, it gets leaked in a breach, and a hacker logs into your Microsoft 365 or VPN. Enforcing MFA on every single remote access point cuts this risk down by 99%.

## 3. Kill the Open RDP (Remote Desktop)
Never expose Remote Desktop Protocol (RDP) directly to the internet. Hackers constantly scan for open RDP ports and brute-force the passwords. If you need remote access, use a secure VPN or a Zero-Trust network overlay like Tailscale.

## 4. Implement Immutable Backups
If ransomware encrypts your server, it will actively seek out your backups and encrypt those too. You need **immutable backups**—backups that are cryptographically locked and cannot be modified or deleted by anyone, even an administrator, for a set period.

## 5. Train Your Team
Your employees are your last line of defense. Regular, engaging security awareness training and simulated phishing tests ensure your team knows how to spot a malicious email before they click the link.
